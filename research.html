Dr. Wang’s Research

Research Overview

Dr. Zhao Wang’s research spans a broad range of interdisciplinary topics centered on cryo–electron microscopy (cryoEM) and cryo–electron tomography (cryoET), with applications in structural biology, biomedical research, and computational method development. His work includes the development of novel cryoEM/cryoET methodologies and software tools, the integration of deep learning approaches for particle detection and data analysis, and the structural and functional characterization of membrane-bound protein complexes.

In addition to methodological advances, Dr. Wang’s research explores translational applications, including early cancer detection strategies and approaches to address antimicrobial resistance through structural insights into drug-resistant systems.

Notably, Dr. Wang contributed to the advancement of in situ cryoET by solving the structure of the AcrAB–TolC multidrug efflux pump within its native cellular context. This work has helped expand opportunities for studying membrane protein complexes in their physiological environment, providing critical insights into structure–function relationships that are difficult to capture using purified systems.
